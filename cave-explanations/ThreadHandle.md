# The Thread Collar

> Cave-cat explanation of the small collar used to identify and inspect a walking cat. Pure analogy, no code tokens.

---

## The Setup

Sometimes more than one doer cat may be walking stones. The cave keeper needs a tiny record for each cat without opening its pouch or changing its walk.

Each cat wears a collar with three marks:

- A unique name for this cat.
- The signpost name where its paw is resting.
- A yes-or-no mark saying whether the cat is still walking.

The collar is only a report. It does not move the cat, pause the cat, or hold the cat's memories.

## Trace

The cave keeper looks across the cave and sees two collars.

- The first says: cat Moon, paw at the entry signpost, still walking.
- The second says: cat Moss, paw at the ending signpost, no longer walking.

The keeper can tell the cats apart and see their reported state. The collars themselves do nothing else.

---

## One Line

A three-mark collar identifies a walking cat, names its current signpost, and says whether it is still active.
